### Our Pledge

We as contributors, leaders, and maintainers pledge to make participation in our community a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, neurodiversity, disability (visible or invisible), ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, political affiliation, or sexual orientation. This includes a commitment to accessibility and inclusivity for those with varied communication styles and needs. We also commit to proactively addressing any form of discrimination, microaggressions, and other biased behaviors, and strive to create a space where everyone feels empowered to participate and contribute. We value diverse perspectives and believe that a welcoming and inclusive environment is essential for fostering innovation and collaboration, especially in the context of security-focused projects where trust and open communication are paramount. We understand that security vulnerabilities can disproportionately impact marginalized groups, and we are committed to creating a community where everyone feels safe and supported in reporting and addressing security concerns. We also acknowledge the importance of protecting whistleblowers who report security vulnerabilities or ethical violations, and those reporting in good faith are protected even if no violation is ultimately found.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community. We expect all members to uphold these standards and contribute to a positive environment for everyone. We recognize that a secure and robust project depends not only on technical excellence, but also on a culture of respect and psychological safety. We are committed to fostering a culture of responsible disclosure and vulnerability management. We encourage community members to report security vulnerabilities through responsible channels and will protect them from retaliation. We are dedicated to addressing security concerns promptly and transparently. We will ensure that individuals with security expertise are involved in addressing security-related reports and concerns.

### Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

*   Being empathetic, patient, and kind toward other people.
*   Respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences, including those based on diverse cultural backgrounds.
*   Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback, focusing on the content and impact of the feedback rather than personal attributes.
*   Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from those experiences to avoid repeating them.
*   Focusing on what is best for the community, prioritizing collective well-being over individual preferences, and considering the potential security implications of all actions and decisions.
*   Using welcoming and inclusive language, avoiding jargon or making assumptions about others' knowledge, and being mindful of how our words may be interpreted by others.
*   Actively listening to understand different perspectives, asking clarifying questions, and demonstrating genuine interest in others' viewpoints.
*   Being mindful of how your communication style may be perceived by others, adapting your approach to ensure clear and respectful communication.
*   Providing alternative formats for content when possible to improve accessibility, such as captions, transcripts, and descriptive text for images.
*   Acknowledging and respecting individuals' boundaries, including requests to cease communication, and respecting their decisions without pressure or judgment.
*   Calling out microaggressions and biased behavior when witnessed and reporting them if they persist, intervening when safe and appropriate to do so.
*   Seeking to understand before reacting or judging, giving others the benefit of the doubt, and approaching situations with curiosity and empathy.
*   Assuming positive intent and giving others the benefit of the doubt, recognizing that misunderstandings can occur and being open to resolving them constructively.
*   Actively promoting a culture of inclusion by mentoring others, sponsoring underrepresented groups, and advocating for equitable policies.
*   Respecting intellectual property rights and adhering to licensing terms, especially regarding cryptographic algorithms and security protocols.
*   Being mindful of the environmental impact of our activities and striving to minimize waste and promote sustainability.
*   Following secure coding practices and promoting awareness of potential security vulnerabilities.
*   Respecting the confidentiality and integrity of sensitive information and data, especially cryptographic keys and sensitive configuration details.
*   Providing timely and accurate information about security vulnerabilities and mitigation strategies to users, in accordance with responsible disclosure practices.
*   Following responsible disclosure practices when reporting security vulnerabilities in third-party software or services.
*   Collaborating with security researchers and other community members to identify and address security vulnerabilities.
*   Promoting the use of strong encryption and authentication mechanisms.
*   Advocating for privacy-enhancing technologies and practices.
*   Respecting the privacy of users and protecting their personal information, in compliance with applicable privacy laws and regulations.
*   Protecting whistleblowers who report security vulnerabilities or ethical violations from retaliation, and ensuring that all reports are handled with confidentiality and impartiality.
*   Adhering to industry best practices for secure software development, vulnerability management, and incident response, including the OWASP guidelines and NIST standards.
*   Participating in security training and awareness programs to stay informed about emerging threats and vulnerabilities.
*   Prioritizing security considerations in all aspects of the project, including design, development, testing, and deployment.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

*   The use of sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances, including persistent or unwanted flirtation, touching, or suggestive comments.
*   Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks, including name-calling, belittling, and other forms of verbal abuse.
*   Public or private harassment of any form, including cyberbullying, online stalking, and threats of violence.
*   Publishing others' private information, such as physical or email addresses, without their explicit permission (doxing), or sharing sensitive information that could compromise their safety or privacy.
*   Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting, including offensive jokes, discriminatory remarks, and any behavior that creates a hostile work environment.
*   Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behavior, or failing to intervene when witnessing such behavior.
*   Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following online or offline, including unwanted surveillance, repeated contact after being asked to stop, and any behavior that creates fear or anxiety.
*   Sustained disruption of discussions or other events, including repeatedly interrupting speakers, dominating conversations, and derailing discussions with irrelevant or off-topic remarks.
*   Making assumptions about others' identities or backgrounds, including misgendering, using incorrect pronouns, or making generalizations about individuals based on their race, ethnicity, or other characteristics.
*   Exclusionary behavior or creating a hostile environment for any member, including gatekeeping, creating cliques, and excluding individuals from opportunities or activities.
*   Continued one-on-one communication after requests to cease, including sending unwanted messages, emails, or other forms of communication.
*   Unwelcome or unsolicited advances or invitations, including persistent requests for dates or other forms of personal interaction.
*   Microaggressions, subtle acts of exclusion, or other biased behaviors, including unintentional slights, snubs, or insults that communicate hostile or negative messages.
*   Gaslighting or manipulative behaviors aimed at undermining an individual's confidence or sense of reality, including denying their experiences, twisting their words, or making them question their sanity.
*   Discrimination based on any protected characteristic, including race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religion, age, disability, or any other characteristic protected by law.
*   Sharing intentionally false or misleading information that could cause harm, including spreading rumors, conspiracy theories, or disinformation that could endanger others.
*   Attributing opinions to others that they do not hold, misrepresenting their views, or using their words out of context to create a false impression.
*   Using technology or other tools to harass, intimidate, or harm others, including creating fake profiles, spreading malicious code, or engaging in other forms of cybercrime.
*   Violating licensing terms or infringing on intellectual property rights, especially regarding cryptographic algorithms and security protocols.
*   Engaging in activities that harm the environment or contribute to climate change, such as excessive energy consumption or promoting unsustainable practices.
*   Misrepresenting your qualifications, experience, or affiliation with the community.
*   Attempting to circumvent security measures or gain unauthorized access to systems or data.
*   Intentionally introducing security vulnerabilities or backdoors into the project's codebase.
*   Disclosing vulnerabilities without following responsible disclosure practices, including failing to provide sufficient time for remediation or disclosing vulnerabilities publicly before a fix is available.
*   Engaging in activities that could compromise the security or integrity of the project or its users, including phishing attacks, denial-of-service attacks, and malware distribution.
*   Ignoring or dismissing security concerns raised by other community members.
*   Discouraging or retaliating against individuals who report security vulnerabilities.
*   Failing to properly handle sensitive data, such as cryptographic keys or user credentials.
*   Using weak or insecure cryptographic algorithms or protocols.
*   Failing to implement appropriate security controls to protect against common attacks, such as SQL injection, cross-site scripting, and buffer overflows.
*   Failing to keep software and systems up-to-date with the latest security patches.
*   Neglecting to perform regular security audits and vulnerability assessments.
*   Engaging in unauthorized penetration testing or other security testing activities.
*   Disclosing confidential information about the project's security infrastructure or incident response procedures.
*   Tampering with or disabling security features or controls.
*   Falsifying or concealing security incidents or vulnerabilities.
*   Failing to comply with relevant security regulations and standards, such as GDPR, HIPAA, and PCI DSS.
*   Retaliating against whistleblowers who report security vulnerabilities or ethical violations.
*   Willfully ignoring security vulnerabilities or failing to take appropriate action to mitigate them.
*   Creating or distributing tools or techniques that could be used to exploit security vulnerabilities.
*   Engaging in activities that violate the privacy of users or compromise their personal information.
*   Failing to implement and maintain a robust security program that addresses all relevant risks and vulnerabilities.
*   Neglecting to implement and enforce secure coding practices throughout the software development lifecycle.
*   Distributing or promoting pirated software or illegal content.
*   Engaging in activities that violate intellectual property rights.

### Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and addressing any violations promptly and fairly. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, discriminatory, or harmful. This includes educating community members about the Code of Conduct, providing guidance on respectful communication, and mediating conflicts when necessary. Special attention will be given to behaviors that could potentially compromise the project's security or disproportionately impact vulnerable community members. Community leaders will receive training on identifying and addressing security-related violations of the Code of Conduct, including security best practices, vulnerability management, and legal and ethical considerations related to data privacy and security. They are also responsible for protecting whistleblowers who report security vulnerabilities or ethical violations from retaliation and ensuring that all reports are handled with confidentiality and impartiality. At least one community leader will have specialized knowledge of security best practices and vulnerability management, and will be responsible for triaging and addressing security-related reports. They will also collaborate with security researchers and other community members to improve the project's security posture. Community leaders are also responsible for fostering a culture of security awareness and promoting secure coding practices within the community. Enforcement actions will be documented and reviewed regularly to ensure consistency and fairness.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, or to temporarily or permanently ban individuals who violate the Code of Conduct. They will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate, and strive to be transparent in their actions, while respecting the privacy of those involved. Decisions will be made with careful consideration and with the goal of fostering a positive and inclusive environment. They will also document all enforcement actions taken, maintaining a record of violations and resolutions. They are also responsible for regularly reviewing and updating the Code of Conduct to ensure that it remains relevant and effective. Community leaders should be trained in conflict resolution, de-escalation techniques, and trauma-informed practices. They should also be aware of relevant legal and ethical considerations, including those related to data privacy and security, and responsible disclosure. At least one community leader will have specialized knowledge of security best practices and vulnerability management, and will be responsible for triaging and addressing security-related reports. They will also collaborate with security researchers and other community members to improve the project's security posture. Community leaders are also responsible for fostering a culture of security awareness and promoting secure coding practices within the community. They will also consult with legal counsel and security experts when necessary to ensure that enforcement actions are appropriate and compliant with applicable laws and regulations. They will also ensure that all enforcement actions are documented and reviewed regularly to ensure consistency and fairness.

### Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, both online and offline, and also applies when an individual is representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event. It also applies to interactions with community members in individual contexts if those interactions stem from their involvement in the community. This includes interactions outside of official community spaces when the behavior impacts the community's overall environment or creates a hostile environment for individual members. This Code of Conduct also extends to interactions with potential new members or those seeking to join the community, as well as interactions with individuals outside the community who are impacted by our activities. This Code of Conduct also applies to archived community spaces and resources. Furthermore, this Code of Conduct applies to all aspects of the project, including code contributions, documentation, testing, security audits, infrastructure, and release management. This Code of Conduct also applies to interactions with third-party vendors and service providers. This Code of Conduct also applies to all communications regarding security vulnerabilities, incident response, and responsible disclosure. This Code of Conduct also applies to any interactions or activities that could potentially impact the security or integrity of the project or its users, regardless of whether those activities occur within official community spaces.

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any concerns, including security vulnerabilities or potential ethical violations, please report it to us. All reports will be treated with discretion, sensitivity, and investigated promptly and fairly. Confidentiality will be maintained to the extent that it does not impede a thorough and fair investigation and is legally permissible. We are committed to protecting the safety and well-being of our community members and fostering a culture of trust and respect. We encourage reporting even if you are unsure whether a violation has occurred. We also encourage you to report any concerns about the conduct of community leaders or enforcement practices. Anonymous reporting is permitted and encouraged where possible, especially for security vulnerabilities or ethical violations. Special care will be taken to protect the anonymity of reporters who are reporting security vulnerabilities, ethical violations, or incidents of harassment. We will prioritize the safety and well-being of the reporter and ensure that they are not subject to retaliation or intimidation. We also commit to protecting whistleblowers who report security vulnerabilities or ethical violations from retaliation. Reports made in good faith, even if ultimately unsubstantiated, will be protected.

We provide multiple reporting methods to ensure accessibility and comfort. Retaliation against anyone who reports a violation of this Code of Conduct is strictly prohibited and will be subject to further disciplinary action. We are also committed to protecting reporters from any form of retaliation or intimidation, including doxing, harassment, or discrimination. We will take steps to ensure that reporters are safe and supported throughout the reporting and investigation process. Support may include providing access to counseling services, legal advice, or other resources. For reports relating to security vulnerabilities, we will provide support in coordinating responsible disclosure and mitigating any potential harm. This support includes working with the reporter to develop a coordinated disclosure plan, providing technical assistance in mitigating the vulnerability, and communicating with affected users. We will also provide legal support and protection to whistleblowers who report security vulnerabilities or ethical violations. We are committed to complying with all applicable whistleblower protection laws. We will also ensure that all reports are handled in compliance with applicable data privacy laws and regulations.

### Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines to determine the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

#### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community. Minor offenses, first-time violations, or unintentional offenses. This also includes minor security lapses that do not pose a significant risk to the project or its users, such as using weak passwords or failing to follow secure coding guidelines. This also includes minor ethical violations that do not involve financial misconduct or harm to individuals.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested, at the discretion of the conduct team and with consideration for the impacted party's preferences and the severity of the offense. Educational resources on inclusive language and respectful communication may also be provided. The individual may be asked to reflect on their behavior and suggest ways to prevent similar incidents in the future. This may also involve a facilitated conversation with the affected party, if all parties are willing and it is deemed appropriate by the conduct team. The conduct team may also require the individual to attend a workshop or training on diversity and inclusion or ethics training. The individual may also be required to write a formal apology to the affected party. For security lapses, the individual may be required to undergo security training or code review training. The individual may also be required to participate in a security awareness program. For ethical violations, the individual may be required to attend ethics training or participate in a compliance review. The warning will be documented in the Code of Conduct enforcement log, including details of the violation, the corrective action taken, and any follow-up actions required.

#### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A more significant violation through a single incident or series of actions, or repeated minor offenses. Persistent or escalating behavior. This also includes more significant security lapses or negligent behavior that could potentially compromise the project's security, such as failing to patch known vulnerabilities or storing sensitive data in plaintext. This also includes more significant ethical violations that could potentially harm individuals or the organization, such as conflicts of interest or violations of privacy.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. This may include a temporary restriction from interacting in specific community spaces (e.g., read-only access to forums, temporary suspension from chat, loss of privileges such as event speaking opportunities). No unsolicited interaction with the people involved, including those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed for a specified period. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban. The violator may also be required to participate in diversity and inclusion training, seek mentorship on respectful communication, or participate in restorative justice practices. The conduct team may also require the individual to make a commitment to specific behavioral changes and set clear expectations for future interactions. A public statement acknowledging the violation and committing to change may also be required. The individual may be required to participate in community service or make a donation to a charity that supports diversity and inclusion or ethical conduct. For security lapses, the individual may be required to undergo a formal security review of their code or contributions. The individual may also be required to implement specific security controls to mitigate the identified vulnerability. The individual may also be required to participate in penetration testing or vulnerability assessment training. For ethical violations, the individual may be required to undergo an ethics review or participate in a compliance audit. The warning will be documented in the Code of Conduct enforcement log, including details of the violation, the warning issued, the consequences of continued behavior, and any follow-up actions required.

#### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, or a pattern of repeated offenses. Actions that cause significant harm or distress to others. This also includes intentional or reckless actions that compromise the security of the project or its users, such as intentionally introducing security vulnerabilities or disclosing sensitive information without authorization. This also includes serious ethical violations that involve financial misconduct, harm to individuals, or violations of law.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban. Upon return, the individual may be subject to probationary status with increased monitoring, required check-ins with the conduct team, and restrictions on their participation. Reinstatement may be contingent on demonstrating a commitment to positive change and ongoing engagement in relevant training or mentorship programs. The individual may also be required to complete a specific set of tasks to demonstrate their commitment to change, such as contributing to diversity and inclusion initiatives or ethics training programs. The individual may be required to write a public apology and participate in a restorative justice process with the affected party. For security-related violations, the individual may be required to participate in a red team exercise to demonstrate their understanding of security vulnerabilities and attack vectors. For ethical violations, the individual may be required to participate in a formal ethics review or compliance investigation. The temporary ban will be documented in the Code of Conduct enforcement log, including details of the violation, the duration of the ban, the conditions for reinstatement, and any follow-up actions required.

#### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals, or a single egregious offense. Actions that cause severe and lasting harm or create a hostile and unsafe environment. This includes intentionally introducing security vulnerabilities, disclosing vulnerabilities irresponsibly, or engaging in malicious activities that could compromise the project's integrity or user data, resulting in significant financial losses, reputational damage, or legal liabilities. This also includes egregious ethical violations that involve widespread financial misconduct, significant harm to individuals, or violations of law that result in legal penalties.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community. The individual's contributions may be removed or anonymized. The ban will be communicated to relevant platforms and communities to prevent further disruptive behavior and protect community members. In cases of severe misconduct, the conduct team may also consider reporting the incident to relevant authorities, including law enforcement agencies and regulatory bodies. The individual may also be publicly identified as having been banned for violating the Code of Conduct, depending on the severity of the offense and the need to protect the community. The individual's name and information may be added to a public list of banned individuals to prevent them from joining other communities. The permanent ban will be documented in the Code of Conduct enforcement log, including details of the violation, the reasons for the permanent ban, and any follow-up actions taken. For security-related violations, the incident may be reported to relevant security communities and law enforcement agencies, and the individual may be subject to legal prosecution. For ethical violations, the incident may be reported to relevant regulatory bodies and law enforcement agencies, and the individual may be subject to legal prosecution and civil penalties. We will also cooperate with law enforcement agencies in their investigations.

### Appeals

Decisions regarding enforcement actions can be appealed by contacting the conduct team via [conduct@example.com](mailto:conduct@example.com). Appeals will be reviewed by a different member of the conduct team than the one who made the original decision, if possible, ensuring impartiality. If an entirely different member is not available, an outside mediator or advisor with expertise in conflict resolution and Code of Conduct enforcement will be consulted. For security-related appeals, a security expert who is not involved in the original decision will be consulted. For ethical violation appeals, a legal expert who is not involved in the original decision will be consulted. The appeal must be submitted within [Number, e.g., 7] days of the enforcement action notification and should clearly state the grounds for the appeal, including any new information or mitigating circumstances that were not previously considered. The conduct team will acknowledge the appeal within [Timeframe, e.g., 24-48 hours] and provide a decision within [Timeframe, e.g., 7-14 days]. The decision on appeal is final, except in extraordinary circumstances where new and compelling evidence emerges that was not previously available. The appeal process may also involve a formal hearing with all parties involved. The appeal decision will be documented in the Code of Conduct enforcement log, including the grounds for the appeal, the review process, and the final decision. If the appeal relates to a security vulnerability report, the appeal process will involve consultation with security experts, and the outcome of the appeal will be communicated to the reporter. If the appeal relates to an ethical violation report, the appeal process will involve consultation with legal counsel, and the outcome of the appeal will be communicated to the reporter. We are committed to providing a fair and impartial appeal process. We will also ensure that the appeal process is conducted in compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

We will also ensure that all reports are handled with confidentiality and impartiality.
